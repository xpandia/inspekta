# INSPEKTA — Video Storyboard

### 60-90 Second Brand/Pitch Video | MunichTech EXPO 2026

---

## CREATIVE DIRECTION

**Tone:** Cinematic precision. German engineering meets Silicon Valley ambition. Every frame deliberate. Every cut intentional. The video should feel like the product: nothing wasted, nothing missed.

**Color Palette:** Dark backgrounds. White typography. Accent color: electric blue (#0066FF) for detection overlays and data visualizations. Red (#FF2D2D) only for defects.

**Music:** Minimal electronic. Clean. Building tension. Think: Arrival soundtrack meets a BMW brand film. No stock music energy. Quiet confidence.

**Typography:** Mono-spaced for data/metrics. Clean sans-serif (Inter or similar) for statements. All caps for single-word impact lines.

**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 primary (with 9:16 vertical cut for social)

---

## SCENE BREAKDOWN

---

### SCENE 1 — THE FLAW [0:00 - 0:12]

**Visual:** Extreme close-up of a precision metal component. Beautiful. Polished. The camera moves slowly across the surface -- macro lens, shallow depth of field. It looks flawless.

**[0:06]** The camera pauses. A hairline fracture becomes visible as the light shifts. Barely there. Almost invisible.

**Text overlay (typed on, mono-spaced):**
```
DEFECT DETECTED
TYPE: STRESS FRACTURE
SIZE: 0.3mm
```

**Audio:** Silence for the first 4 seconds. Then a single, low-frequency tone. Subtle. Like a heartbeat.

**Voiceover (calm, measured, male or female -- German-accented English):**
> "This fracture is 0.3 millimeters wide."

---

### SCENE 2 — THE COST [0:12 - 0:22]

**Visual:** Quick cuts. Each lasts 1.5 seconds. Each paired with a data point that slams onto screen:

1. A car recall notice in a newspaper headline -- **$3.1 TRILLION**
2. A stopped production line, red warning lights spinning -- **LOST EVERY YEAR**
3. An exhausted quality inspector rubbing their eyes under fluorescent lights -- **TO MANUFACTURING DEFECTS**

**Voiceover:**
> "The human eye misses 20% of defects. The cost: three point one trillion dollars a year."

**Audio:** Each cut punctuated by a sharp, clean percussion hit. Building rhythm.

---

### SCENE 3 — THE REVEAL [0:22 - 0:35]

**Visual:** Black screen. Beat of silence.

**[0:23]** The Inspekta logo assembles on screen. Clean. Precise. Like a technical blueprint being drawn.

**[0:25]** Cut to: a production line from above. Parts moving. An Inspekta camera module is mounted above -- sleek, compact, industrial. A blue scanning line sweeps across each part.

**Visual overlay:** Real-time bounding boxes appear around each component. Green for pass. The system is working. Fast. Silent. Relentless.

**[0:30]** A defective part enters. Red bounding box snaps around it. The part is diverted. The line never stops.

**Text overlay:**
```
99.7% ACCURACY
< 200ms RESPONSE
24/7 OPERATION
```

**Voiceover:**
> "Inspekta. AI-powered visual inspection that catches what humans cannot. In real-time. Every part. Every time."

**Audio:** Music builds -- layered synths, restrained but confident.

---

### SCENE 4 — THE INTELLIGENCE [0:35 - 0:50]

**Visual:** Transition to the Inspekta dashboard. Dark UI, data flowing. The camera pushes in slowly.

**[0:36]** Defect classification panel populates: scratch, fracture, dimensional anomaly. Each with a confidence score.

**[0:40]** Analytics view: a trend line showing defect rate over 48 hours. The system highlights an emerging pattern -- tool wear correlation. A predictive alert fires.

**[0:45]** Split screen: left side shows the dashboard alert, right side shows a factory operator receiving the notification on a rugged tablet. They nod. Walk to the machine. Adjust the die. Problem solved before it escalated.

**Text overlay:**
```
DETECT. CLASSIFY. PREDICT.
```

**Voiceover:**
> "It does not just find defects. It finds their cause. And it warns you before the next one happens."

**Audio:** Music reaches a controlled peak. Not loud -- precise.

---

### SCENE 5 — THE SCALE [0:50 - 1:00]

**Visual:** Pull back from a single camera to reveal an entire factory floor. Twelve Inspekta units mounted across multiple lines. Blue scanning lines sweeping in synchronized rhythm. A digital nervous system watching everything.

**[0:55]** Overlay: a world map. Dots light up across Germany, then Europe, then globally. Data flowing between them.

**Text overlay (large, centered):**
```
ONE CAMERA.
ONE LINE OF SIGHT.
ZERO DEFECTS.
```

**Voiceover:**
> "One camera per line. Minutes to deploy. Built to scale from a single station to every factory on earth."

**Audio:** Music simplifies. Returns to the clean, singular tone from the opening.

---

### SCENE 6 — THE CLOSE [1:00 - 1:15]

**Visual:** Return to the extreme close-up from Scene 1. The same metal component. The same surface.

But this time, no fracture. Perfect. The camera lingers.

**[1:05]** Cut to black.

**[1:07]** The Inspekta logo. Clean white on black.

**Tagline appears below:**

> **See What Humans Miss. Fix What Machines Can't.**

**[1:10]** Final card:

```
inspekta.ai
MunichTech EXPO 2026
```

**Voiceover (final line, quiet intensity):**
> "Inspekta does not blink."

**Audio:** Single tone sustains. Fades to silence. Clean cut to black.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Shot List Priority

| Scene | Footage Type | Source |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Macro product shot | Shoot (or high-quality stock) |
| 2 | Factory/recall imagery | Licensed stock footage |
| 3 | Product line + Inspekta unit | 3D render + screen capture of real UI |
| 4 | Dashboard UI | Screen recording of working prototype |
| 5 | Factory wide shot | Licensed stock + composited Inspekta units |
| 6 | Macro product shot (reprise) | Same shoot as Scene 1 |

### Minimum Viable Version (Hackathon Cut)

If full production is not feasible before the event, build the video from:

1. **Stock footage** of precision manufacturing (Artgrid, Shutterstock)
2. **Screen recordings** of the actual Inspekta dashboard prototype
3. **Motion graphics** for detection overlays and data visualizations (After Effects or Motion)
4. **AI voiceover** (ElevenLabs -- professional, measured tone) or record in-person
5. **Music** from Artlist or Epidemic Sound -- search: "minimal cinematic technology"

This approach can produce a broadcast-quality 75-second video in 48 hours.

### Aspect Ratio Deliverables

| Format | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| 16:9 (1920x1080) | Stage presentation, YouTube, website |
| 9:16 (1080x1920) | Instagram Reels, TikTok, LinkedIn Stories |
| 1:1 (1080x1080) | LinkedIn feed, Twitter/X |

### Pacing Rules

- No shot longer than 4 seconds
- No text on screen longer than 3 seconds
- Every cut must have purpose -- advance the narrative or reveal new information
- The video should feel like it is moving forward at all times, even in quiet moments
